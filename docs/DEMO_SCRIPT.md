# Prism AI Adaptation Studio - Demo Script

**Target Duration:** 2:30 - 2:50 minutes
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover narration

---

## 1. Opening (0:00 - 0:15)

### Narration
"Prism AI Adaptation Studio helps creators adapt stories for different formats and platforms. Traditional generative tools often skip creator intent, traceability, and creative tradeoffs. Prism solves this by giving creators transparent recommendations and full control over every decision."

### On-Screen Action
- Show Prism landing page
- Highlight the tagline and main interface

### Recording Notes
- Keep pace steady and clear
- Emphasize "transparent" and "full control"

---

## 2. Source Story (0:15 - 0:30)

### Narration
"Let's start with an original story. I'll click 'Load Demo Story' to load 'The Last Bloom,' a short science fiction piece included with Prism. The default runtime is 15 minutes, and we're targeting a short film for festival, streaming, or social media platforms."

### On-Screen Action
- Click "Load Demo Story" button
- Show story title "The Last Bloom" appearing
- Briefly show target runtime (15 minutes)
- Show target medium (Short Film)
- Show platform options (Festival / Streaming / Social Media)

### Recording Notes
- Pause briefly after loading to let viewers see the story title
- Don't read the full story text
- Clearly show the 15-minute runtime

---

## 3. Creative DNA (0:30 - 0:55)

### Narration
"Now I'll click 'Analyze Story' to extract the Creative DNA. In live mode, this uses IBM Granite 4 H Small through watsonx.ai. For this recorded demo, you'll see visibly labeled pre-generated fixtures because the trial inference quota is unavailable. Either way, all outputs are schema-validated before display."

### On-Screen Action
- Click "Analyze Story" button
- Show loading state briefly
- Display Creative DNA Summary card
- Expand one section (e.g., "Core Themes" or "Narrative Structure")
- Scroll to show validation indicators

### Recording Notes
- Clearly state "visibly labeled pre-generated fixtures" and "trial quota unavailable"
- Point out the schema validation mention in the UI
- Don't rush through the Creative DNA display
- Do not imply fixtures are live AI output

---

## 4. Adaptation Recommendations (0:55 - 1:30)

### Narration
"Next, I'll generate adaptation recommendations. Each recommendation shows proposed changes, reasoning, creative benefits, potential risks, and a confidence score. This is where Prism differs from typical AI tools—it doesn't silently decide for you. I'll accept the first three recommendations, reject the next two, and leave the sixth pending to show how creators maintain control."

### On-Screen Action
- Click "Generate Recommendations" button
- Show recommendations loading
- Display recommendation cards
- Accept recommendation 1 (click Accept button, show green checkmark)
- Accept recommendation 2
- Accept recommendation 3
- Reject recommendation 4 (click Reject button, show red X)
- Reject recommendation 5
- Leave recommendation 6 pending (no action)
- Show the mixed state of accepted/rejected/pending

### Recording Notes
- Briefly hover over one recommendation to show the full details
- Emphasize the Accept/Reject buttons
- Show confidence scores clearly
- Demonstrate that the user is making conscious choices
- Follow the exact pattern: 1-3 accepted, 4-5 rejected, 6 pending

---

## 5. Production Blueprint (1:30 - 2:10)

### Narration
"With my decisions made, I'll generate the pre-production blueprint. The Overview tab shows the complete structure and includes the Creative Handoff summary. The Story Plan tab shows individual scenes with full traceability back to the source. Creative Direction provides guidance for the production team. And Production Review highlights feasibility concerns and creative risks."

### On-Screen Action
- Click "Generate Production Blueprint" button
- Show loading state
- Display Overview tab (show scene count, duration, key elements)
- Scroll to show Creative Handoff within Overview
- Click Story Plan tab
- Expand one scene to show source traceability
- Click Creative Direction tab (show briefly)
- Click Production Review tab (show feasibility and risks)

### Recording Notes
- Move through tabs at a steady pace
- Highlight that Creative Handoff is within Overview, not a separate tab
- Emphasize traceability in Story Plan
- Show feasibility scores in Production Review
- Only show the four tabs: Overview, Story Plan, Creative Direction, Production Review

---

IBM Bob, the AI coding assistant, contributed substantially to this project: scaffolding, schemas, prompts, API routes, validation and repair logic, UI components, debugging, refactoring, demo fixtures, and repository housekeeping. This collaboration demonstrates how AI can accelerate development while maintaining quality and structure."

### On-Screen Action

### Recording Notes
- Keep this segment factual and specific
- List Bob's contributions accurately
- Maintain professional tone

---

## 7. Closing (2:35 - 2:50)

### Narration
"Prism transforms the adaptation workflow: from source story to Creative DNA, to explainable recommendations, to human decisions, to a pre-production blueprint. AI supports creative judgment—it doesn't replace it. That's Prism."

### On-Screen Action
- Show final Production Blueprint on screen
- Fade to Prism logo or landing page

### Recording Notes
- Deliver closing with confidence
- End on a strong visual
- Keep final line concise and memorable

## Key Messages

1. **Problem:** Traditional AI tools lack transparency and creator control
2. **Solution:** Prism provides explainable recommendations and human decision points
3. **Technology:** IBM Granite 4 H Small via watsonx.ai (live mode)
4. **Transparency:** Demo mode uses visibly labeled pre-generated fixtures
5. **Validation:** All outputs are schema-validated
6. **Control:** Creators accept, reject, or defer every recommendation
7. **Output:** Pre-production blueprint with full traceability
8. **Collaboration:** IBM Bob contributed substantially to development

## What NOT to Say

- ❌ "This will revolutionize filmmaking"
- ❌ "AI can replace human creativity"
- ❌ "Future features include..." (only show what exists)
- ❌ "The demo fixtures are live AI output" (be transparent about demo mode)
- ❌ "This is production-ready" (say "pre-production blueprint")
- ❌ "This is the best adaptation tool" (let the demo speak)
- ❌ Exaggerated claims about capabilities
- ❌ Promises about production use without caveats
